## What Makes a POS System Right for a Café?

Café POS requirements differ from general retail. Here's what matters specifically for coffee shops and small food service:

### Speed at the counter
Morning rush hour means customers queuing. A POS with a cluttered UI, slow loading, or mandatory internet round-trips will slow you down at the worst possible time. Look for:
- Large, tap-friendly product buttons on a 10" screen
- One or two taps to add a product to the cart
- Quick payment — scan, tap, or cash entry with instant change calculation
- Fast receipt printing (USB thermal, not Bluetooth)

### Modifier support
"Extra shot," "oat milk instead of full cream," "no sugar" — café orders are full of modifications. Your POS must support modifier groups attached to products, not just free-text notes.

Good modifier implementation:
- Modifier groups (milk type, shot count, temperature, syrup)
- Optional vs. required modifiers
- Price adjustments on modifiers (extra shot = +$0.50)
- Modifier display on kitchen ticket and receipt

### Hold orders
If you have even two items being prepared simultaneously, hold order support is essential. A cashier should be able to take the next customer's order while the barista finishes the previous drink.

### Offline operation
Café internet connections fail. A shared Wi-Fi router dropping during the morning rush will kill your sales if your POS is cloud-dependent. An offline-first POS continues processing orders from local storage and syncs when connectivity returns.

### Basic inventory for ingredients (optional but useful)
Cafés don't always track ingredient-level inventory in their POS — but tracking sellable items (bags of coffee beans, packaged pastries, bottled water) is useful for ordering and shrinkage control.

## Setting Up ZyntaPOS for a Coffee Shop: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Install and create account

Download ZyntaPOS on your Android tablet. On first launch, the setup wizard asks for:
- Business name
- Currency (supports all major currencies)
- Tax settings (VAT/GST rate, inclusive or exclusive)
- Admin PIN

### Step 2: Build your menu

Create categories first:
- Coffee (Hot)
- Coffee (Iced)
- Tea
- Cold Drinks
- Food / Pastries
- Retail (bagged coffee, merchandise)

Then add products within each category. For drinks with modifiers, create modifier groups:
- **Milk:** Full cream, Skim, Oat (+$0.50), Almond (+$0.50), Soy (+$0.50)
- **Size:** Small, Medium, Large (with price difference)
- **Extras:** Extra shot (+$0.50), Decaf, Sugar-free syrup (+$0.30)

Attach modifier groups to relevant products.

### Step 3: Connect the receipt printer

Settings → Printer Setup → select your printer model → connect via USB cable.
Print a test receipt to confirm connection.

### Step 4: Configure cash drawer

If using a cash drawer connected to the receipt printer (standard RJ11 connection), enable the drawer-kick setting in printer configuration.

### Step 5: Set up staff

Add staff accounts with cashier role. Staff can process sales and access basic reports. Manager role adds stock management and detailed reporting access.

### Step 6: Open register and start selling

Dashboard → Open Register → enter opening cash amount → tap Start.

Products appear in a grid layout. Tap to add to cart. Tap the cart to checkout. Select payment type (cash, card, split). Enter amount → Change calculated automatically → Print receipt.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Café POS

**Tip 1: Use the loyalty programme from day one.** Even simple points-per-purchase builds return visits. Customer loyalty accounts in ZyntaPOS track purchase history and points automatically.

**Tip 2: Check daily sales every morning.** The previous day's sales summary shows your top products, busiest hours, and payment method breakdown. Use it to adjust staffing, ordering, and promotions.

**Tip 3: Set low stock alerts on high-turnover items.** Oat milk, coffee beans, specific pastries — set a reorder point so you never run out during service.

**Tip 4: Use a keyboard barcode scanner for retail items.** If you sell packaged items (ground coffee, merchandise), a USB barcode scanner speeds up checkout significantly.

**Tip 5: Run the morning shift on a fresh register session.** Open Register at the start of each shift, close at the end. The register reconciliation shows exactly how much cash should be in the drawer versus what actually is.
