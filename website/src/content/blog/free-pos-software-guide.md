"Free POS software" is one of the most searched terms in the retail technology space — and one of the most misleading. Dozens of platforms advertise as free, then make their money through transaction fees, mandatory hardware purchases, or paywalled features that any real business needs.

This guide exposes the real cost of "free" POS systems and identifies the six options that are genuinely free to use, with no strings attached.

---

## The Truth About "Free" POS Software

There are three business models disguised as "free":

### Model 1: Freemium (free tier with paid upgrades)
The software is free, but essential features — inventory management, multi-user access, reports, barcode scanning — require a paid plan. You can start free but you'll hit the ceiling fast.

**Examples:** Loyverse, iZettle, SumUp

### Model 2: Free software, paid processing
The app is free, but all card payments route through the vendor's payment processor at 2–3% per transaction. The software cost is subsidised by your revenue.

**Examples:** Square, PayPal Zettle, SumUp

### Model 3: Genuinely free
The software is free. It may have premium tiers, but the free tier is functional for real business use. Revenue comes from subscriptions, not your transactions.

**Examples:** ZyntaPOS, Loyverse (with caveats), Wave POS

Understanding which model a vendor uses tells you the real cost of "free."

### 3. Square POS — Best Free POS for Card-First Businesses

**Free tier includes:** POS app, basic inventory, basic reporting, invoicing, online store (limited)

**Hidden costs:** 2.6% + 10¢ per card swipe. Requires Square card reader hardware ($49+). Advanced inventory and retail features: $60+/month.

**Offline:** ⚠️ Limited — stores card payments offline but many features require internet

**Hardware:** Square card reader required; any iOS or Android device

Square's free tier is polished and the onboarding is excellent. But transaction fees make it expensive at volume. A business processing $15,000/month in cards pays Square ~$390/month in fees — $4,680/year.

If you process mostly cash, Square's fee model hurts less. If cards are your main payment method, calculate the real annual cost before committing.

**Best for:** New businesses starting out, craft sellers, mobile vendors with low-to-medium transaction volume.

**Best for:** Freelancers, consultants, and service businesses that primarily use invoicing rather than a traditional checkout.

## What "Free" Features Every Business Actually Needs

Before choosing a free POS, verify these features work on the free tier:

**Must-have on free:**
- Unlimited products (or at least 500+)
- Inventory quantity tracking
- Receipt printing
- Cash and basic payment handling
- Multi-user login (at least 2 users)
- Daily sales summary report
- Offline operation

**Nice-to-have on free:**
- Customer profiles
- Discount and coupon support
- Low-stock alerts
- Export to CSV

If any must-have is behind a paywall, reconsider whether the platform is truly free for your use case.

## How to Switch to a Free POS Without Losing Data

Moving from a paid POS to a free one is straightforward if you plan the migration:

1. **Export your product catalogue** from the current system (CSV is standard)
2. **Export customer data** if you have a loyalty programme
3. **Note your current settings:** tax rates, currency, receipt header/footer
4. **Install and configure the new system** during a slow period (Tuesday morning, etc.)
5. **Import products** via CSV import in the new POS
6. **Run parallel for one day** — process sales on both, verify totals match
7. **Cut over** on your next business day
